About This Site

I am obsessed with Mount Rainier. It is my favorite place to hike, and my spirit mountain. When I first moved to the Pacific Northwest I wrote to friends:

"When I wake up and see Rainier on the horizon, I think yeah, this is going to be a good day. In fact this would be a good mountain to worship: a pagan god of rock and ice and strangeness."

In the fall of 2018 I realized three things in rapid succession:

  1. I want to someday hike the Wonderland Trail.
  2. Before attempting such a thing I needed to broaden my horizons beyond day hiking, to gain backpacking experience on shorter trips.
  3. Actually, I want to hike EVERY maintained trail inside Mount Rainier National Park.

This website serves as a record of the wonderful sights and memories I am experiencing along the way to my Rainier goal. I'm recording the trails I hike for personal satisfaction, and publishing these records in case the things I've learned might prove useful to other Rainier lovers.

The map on the homepage highlights all the trails I have visited so far, colored according to which year I first hiked them. Move your mouse over a trail name to highlight where that trail is located. Clicking a trail name leads to a page with notes about that trail, plus a close-up map which can be clicked again for a larger version. Many thanks to the National Park Service for making the background map I based these on available as public domain.

My difficulty rating are entirely subjective, based on my own experience of each trail. Be warned that I tend to enjoy long slow hikes, so I judge difficulty more by elevation change or other terrain challenges than raw distance. If you prefer steep-but-short to long hauls, take my "moderate" ratings with a pinch of salt :-) Also note that I'm not creating separate entries for all the shorter trails, because who can be bothered to drive all the way to the mountain and back just to hike a couple of miles? Instead I will roll information about short routes into whatever article covers a longer nearby trail: for instance the Trail of the Shadows loop is described as part of Rampart Ridge.

I measured elevations and distances using http://caltopo.com. This tends to produce slightly larger values than are published elsewhere. One of the many things I love about Rainier is how you can look up the length of a trail in 5 different places, and get 5 different answers!

If you want to get in touch, Twitter is the best way to reach me.
