About This Site

I am obsessed with Mount Rainier. It is my favorite place to hike, and my spirit mountain. When I first moved to the Pacific Northwest I wrote to friends:

"When I wake up and see Rainier on the horizon, I think yeah, this is going to be a good day. In fact this would be a good mountain to worship: a pagan god of rock and ice and strangeness."

In the fall of 2018 I realized three things in rapid succession:

  1. I want to someday hike the Wonderland Trail.
  2. Before attempting such a thing I needed to broaden my horizons beyond day hiking, gaining backpacking experience on shorter trips.
  3. Actually, I want to hike EVERY maintained trail inside Mount Rainier National Park.

This website serves as a personal record of the wonderful places I am experiencing along the way to my Rainier goal. I'm keeping track of the trails I hike for my own satisfaction, and publishing these records in case the things I'm learning might prove useful to other Rainier lovers.

The map on the homepage highlights all the trails I have visited so far, colored according to which year I first hiked each section of trail. Move your mouse over a trail name to highlight where it is located. Clicking a trail name leads to a page with notes about that trail, plus a close-up map which can be clicked again for a larger version. Many thanks to the National Park Service for making the high resolution map these are based on available as public domain!

Hopefully needless to say: please do not rely on these maps for navigation. They aim to inform, but are insufficient to keep you un-lost.

My difficulty ratings are highly subjective based on my own experience of each trail. Be warned that I tend to enjoy long, slow hikes, so I judge difficulty more by elevation change or other terrain challenges than raw distance. If you prefer steep-but-short over long hauls, take my easy and moderate ratings with a pinch of salt :-) Also note that I'm not creating separate entries for shorter trails, because who can be bothered to drive all the way to the mountain and back just to hike a couple of miles? Instead I will roll information about short routes into whatever article covers a longer nearby trail: for instance the Trail of the Shadows is described here as part of Rampart Ridge.

I measured elevations and distances using http://caltopo.com. This tends to produce slightly larger values than are published elsewhere. One of the many things I love about Rainier is how you can look up the length of a trail in 5 different places, and get 5 different answers!

No apologies for the resolutely early 2000's style of this website. I am a hiker and software engineer, but not a web designer :-)

I can be contacted via Twitter, plus I'm usually hanging out in the Wonderland Trail Facebook group.
