about Cougar Rock Cougar Rock is a frontcountry car campground, so it doesn't use the Rainier backcountry campsite reservation system. |
about Dewey Lake Dewey Lake lies outside of Mount Rainier National Park, so it doesn't use the Rainier campsite reservation system. It's included as an option here because camping somewhere along that section of the PCT can be useful for making a loop by connecting the Laughingwater Creek trail at Three Lakes to the northern end of the Eastside Trail at Tipsoo Lake. |
about Longmire There are no campsites at Longmire, but you could book a night at the National Park Inn if you fancy staying here to enjoy a hot shower and restaurant meal. |
about alternate routes If there is more than one route between two locations, this page will measure the shortest one, except for trail segments in the northwest corner of the park (around Mowich Lake / Ipsut Pass / Spray Park / Carbon River) where choice of route is determined by the via Spray Park option.

For finer control over alternate route selection, you can choose a different via option for each day of your trip.

To check which way was measured, move your mouse over or tap on the distance for a day, and you'll see a tooltip listing all the locations along that day's trail. |
about the Auto buttons Clicking the Auto CW or Auto CCW button will generate a suggested clockwise or counter-clockwise Wonderland itinerary using your current trip duration, start trailhead, finish trailhead, and via Spray Park settings. Campsites will be spaced as evenly as possible around the loop in the chosen direction.

This feature only supports full or partial Wonderland circuits, not other trails such as the Northern Loop or Eastside Loop. If you pick trailheads that are not on the Wonderland, it will choose a route that connects from them to the Wonderland.
